The University of Pittsburgh is a major research university located in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Founded in 1787, it's one of the oldest universities in the United States and is known for its strong programs in engineering, medicine, and business. Pitt is part of the University of Pittsburgh system and is consistently ranked among the top public universities nationally. The campus features the iconic Cathedral of Learning, a 42-story Gothic Revival building that serves as the university's most recognizable landmark. The university is also home to notable research centers and has a significant presence in healthcare through its medical school and UPMC (University of Pittsburgh Medical Center) partnerships.
